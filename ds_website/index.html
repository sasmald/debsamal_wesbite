// HOW I LEARN A DOMAIN

I don't start with the product. I start with the room.

Rapid domain acquisition is the skill that lets me move between markets. Here's the sequence I run every time.

01
Map the experts and their incentives

I learn who holds the knowledge, who decides, who pays, and who quietly blocks. In expert-heavy markets, the org chart lies and the incentive map doesn't.

02
Hunt the constraint, not the feature

Almost every product I've shipped won because I found the real bottleneck (specialist time, onboarding friction, a broken handoff) and built against that.

03
Write a thesis I can be wrong about

I put a falsifiable thesis on the table early and let domain experts stress-test it. The fastest way to learn a market is to be wrong in front of people who know better, then fix it.

04
Decide build / buy / partner from the thesis

Once the constraint is clear, the path usually is too. My job is to make that call honestly, even when it's inconvenient for the org's default.

I'm running this same sequence right now on Keystone AI, an agentic build system gated by exactly this kind of judgment call at every stage.
