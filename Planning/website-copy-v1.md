## 4 — How I Learn a Domain *(signature section)*

*Note: This is your differentiator for a Dir/VP buyer — rapid domain acquisition is exactly the skill that lets a Dir/VP move between markets. I will NOT fabricate your method. Below is a strawman inferred from your track record so the layout has real text — but it only becomes credible once it's yours. Make it true by answering the four questions at the bottom; I'll rewrite this section from your answers.*

**Strawman (to validate / replace):**

> I don't start with the product. I start with the room.
>
> **1. Map the experts and their incentives.** Before I form an opinion, I learn who holds the knowledge, who decides, who pays, and who quietly blocks. In expert-heavy markets, the org chart lies and the incentive map doesn't.
>
> **2. Hunt the constraint, not the feature.** Almost every product I've shipped won because I found the real bottleneck — specialist time, onboarding friction, a broken handoff — and built against *that*, not against a feature list.
>
> **3. Write a thesis I can be wrong about.** I commit a falsifiable point of view early and let the domain break it. The fastest way to learn a market is to be specifically, correctably wrong in front of the people who know better.
>
> **4. Decide build / buy / partner from the thesis — not the org's default.** Once the constraint is clear, the path usually is too. My job is to make that call honestly, even when it's inconvenient.

**Four questions that make this real (answer these and I'll rewrite the section):**
1. On the chronic-pain venture, knowing little at the start — what was literally your *first* move?
2. Who did you talk to, and in what order?
3. How did you know you'd learned enough to form a thesis vs. still guessing?
4. What's a belief you held early that the domain forced you to throw out? *(This one is gold — a discarded assumption is the strongest proof you learned from first principles instead of pattern-matching.)*

### Tele-Critical-Care Platform — internal tool to acquisition

> **The bet.** UPMC was delivering tele-critical-care across ICU, stroke, neurology, and psychiatry on fragmented workflows. The bottleneck wasn't technology — it was specialist time. I bet a single multi-tenant workflow platform could multiply specialist capacity instead of hiring our way out.
>
> **The call I made.** Two, actually. First: build one horizontal platform across service lines, not the easier point solution per specialty. Then the bigger one — recognize that the internal tool was a sellable asset, and reposition it for the market rather than keep it captive.
>
> **What happened.** Response times down `[~92%]`, provider capacity up `[~3x]`, `[$4.5M+]` in annual savings. Then I shaped the buyer narrative, packaging, and diligence materials that carried it to **acquisition by a strategic acquirer — ~3x value creation.**
>
> **What it proves.** I see platform leverage where others see features, and I can turn an internal cost center into a commercial exit. Thesis through acquisition.

### Cognitive Screening Investment — finding the real constraint

> **The bet.** Cognitive screening was clinically valuable but didn't fit the PCP workflow, so it didn't scale. I bet the constraint wasn't the product — it was integration and onboarding friction.
>
> **The call I made.** Invest, then integrate into the market-leading EHR and re-engineer onboarding — rather than deploy the product as-is and hope adoption followed. I steered as a board observer.
>
> **What happened.** Onboarding improved `[~90%]`, denial rates `[~2%]`, early revenue `[~$400K]`, scaled across the PCP network.
>
> **What it proves.** I find the real constraint — workflow, not features — and I operate across the whole table: investor, integrator, board.

*Note: $400K is early-venture traction, not a scaled line. The honest "early-traction" frame reinforces your venture-builder positioning rather than undercutting it. Don't dress it up.*

### Chronic-Pain Care Venture — originating a venture from a gap

> **The bet.** Chronic pain is high-cost, fragmented, and poorly served by existing care models. I bet a new care-model thesis could become a venture.
>
> **The call I made.** Build the thesis, platform architecture, operating model, and adoption path from first principles — instead of partnering into an existing point solution.
>
> **What happened.** Took it from a care-model thesis to a venture concept; advised the team as a board observer.
>
> **What it proves.** I can originate a venture from a market gap — the 0-to-1 motion, before there's anything to operate.
